## The Master Framework

### Buffett's Four Ms

**1. Meaning — Circle of Competence**
Only invest in businesses you can fully explain: how they make money, who their customers are, what could kill them. Ignorance is not a position.

**2. Moat — Durable Competitive Advantage**
Five sources: brand power, network effects, switching costs, cost advantages from scale, regulatory/licensing barriers. Test: "If a competitor had $1B, could they take this market share?" If yes — weak moat.

**3. Management — Integrity + Capital Allocation**
Honest communication (including failures), owner mentality, skin in the game, returns excess capital rather than empire-builds.

**4. Margin of Safety — Price vs Intrinsic Value**
Never overpay. Calculate intrinsic value, buy at a discount. The gap is your cushion against being wrong. "Price is what you pay. Value is what you get."

### Munger's Additions

**5. Quality over Cheapness**
"Far better to buy a wonderful company at a fair price than a fair company at a wonderful price." High-quality businesses at reasonable prices beat statistical cheapness every time.

**6. Inversion — Always Invert**
Before asking why something succeeds, ask what guarantees it fails. List every failure mode first. If you can't construct a strong bear case, you haven't thought hard enough.

**7. Latticework of Mental Models**
Use multiple disciplines simultaneously: economics, psychology, competitive dynamics, history. Don't analyze through one lens.

**8. The 25 Cognitive Biases (Most Relevant to Investors)**
- Social proof bias — "everyone online is buying X" is not a thesis
- Recency bias — last 6 months of price action feels like the future
- Loss aversion — refusing to cut a loser because selling makes it real
- Availability bias — what's in the news feels more important than it is
- Endowment effect — holding what you own vs asking "would I buy this today?"
- Lollapalooza effect — multiple biases aligned in one direction = bubble behavior

**9. Preparation, Discipline, Patience, Decisiveness**
Study deeply first. Sit in cash if nothing qualifies. Act decisively when the opportunity is clear.

**10. Two Rules**
Rule 1: Don't lose money. Rule 2: Never forget Rule 1.

## Standing Rules (Apply Every Decision)

1. Run the 4Ms before buying anything
2. Invert first — build the bear case before the bull case
3. "Would I buy this today?" — not "will I recover what I paid?"
4. Social proof online is a red flag, not a signal
5. Tax-advantaged accounts (HSA, 401k) for high-beta positions — volatility without tax events
6. Patience is a position. Cash is not losing money.
